TAIOIA XXXIV

A stove made up of fragments found in the Dominican monastery in Nuremberg, described on page 9, Plate XXIII of this work.

This magnificent utensil was restored by the Author, and excellent copies in various colors can be obtained from the earthenware manufacturers Metzger and Gruber in Nuremberg for the price of 70 to 88 florins.

The entire stove is ten feet tall.

TABLE XXXV
Fig. a.
This is a description of the baptistery that once belonged to the church of Grimmenlhal, a once famous pilgrimage site but now destroyed.
The architect and ornamentalist Giovanni Eberlein, one of my pupils, gave me this design.
This baptismal font, evidently from the time when the church was founded, is simple, executed in the primitive, but pure style.
The base is square, as indicated in the plan.
From this base rises an octagon, the eight upper sides of which represent the symbols and attributes of the Evangelists, Christ on the cross, the Virgin and Saint John, all adorned with arabesques.
The eight lower sides are covered with images of animals curiously linked, and the tiles in the middle contain the images of Saint Cyriacus, Saint Giles, Saint Catherine, Saint Margaret, the Madonna and Child, Saint George, Saint Blaise and Saint Erasmus.
By order of the Duke, this curious baptistery must be carefully restored and given an appropriate purpose.
Interesting details were taken from the stalls of the choir in the chapel of St. George at Altemburg Castle, the residence of the duke of that name.
These details were relayed to me by Schmidt, architect at the court of Altemburg, to whom the conservation of several beautiful monuments is owed.
